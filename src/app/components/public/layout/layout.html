About the Mala Jangam Online Communtiy

The Jangam (or Jangama) is a Shaivite community serving as priests, gurus, and wandering monks, primarily in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Karnataka. Known as "moving lingas" and devotees of Shiva, they traditionally conduct rituals for non-Brahmin Veershaiva households, play musical instruments, and sometimes practice begging for livelihood.

Key Aspects of the Jangam Community

While rooted in spiritual traditions, modern economic pressures have led some members to take up other jobs, including street vending, in addition to their traditional priestly functions.

Role and Philosophy

They are considered sacred, with their visits to homes seen as a visit by Lord Shiva. They are the custodians of Veerashaiva culture and often act as educators on the teachings of Basava.

Lifestyle

Traditionally, they are nomadic, moving in small groups, or village-based priests, though many have transitioned to agriculture or government jobs.

Regional Variations

In some regions, they are referred to as Jangam Brahmins, holding a high status within the Shaiva sect.

Sub-castes

The Budega Jangam (or Beda Jangam) is a notable group, often associated with, and sometimes listed under, Scheduled Castes (SCs) in states like Karnataka.

Cultural Practices

They are known for performing the Veeragase dance and using instruments like the Ganta (bell).
