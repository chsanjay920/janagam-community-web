About the Mala Jangam Online Communtiy

The Jangam (or Jangama) is a Shaivite community serving as priests, gurus, and wandering monks, primarily in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Karnataka. Known as "moving lingas" and devotees of Shiva, they traditionally conduct rituals for non-Brahmin Veershaiva households, play musical instruments, and sometimes practice begging for livelihood.

Key Aspects of the Jangam Community

Jangams are a community of itinerant Shaivite priests, monks, and mendicants in India, particularly prominent in the north (Haryana, Uttar Pradesh) and South (Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh). Known as “moving linga” or walking embodiments of Lord Shiva, they are deeply associated with the Lingayat / Veerashaiva tradition and perform rituals, narrate stories, and live off alms.

Role & Function

They act as religious gurus, priests, and keepers of traditions within Shaivism, often regarded as living, walking representations of Shiva.

Lifestyle

Traditionally wandering monks, they travel across various states, including Haryana, Punjab, Rajasthan, and Uttar Pradesh, particularly during the wedding season of Shiva and Parvati in Phalgun (February–March).

Culture & Performance

Known for Jangam Gayan (narrative singing) and Jangam Veeragase dance, they perform at Shiva temples to large gatherings.

Background

While some are associated with the Veerashaiva-Lingayat sect, others like the Budega Jangam are recognized as a separate community in Southern India.

Location

They are mainly concentrated in northern India, especially in the Kurukshetra and Thanesar areas of Haryana.

Key Concepts Related to Jangam

The Jangam community continues to be an important part of the Shaivite tradition, contributing to the religious and cultural landscape of India.

Jangama Dhyana

A meditation technique developed by the community.

Jangam Gayan

A narrative singing tradition, often performed in the courtyards of Shiva temples.

Jangam Veeragase

A vigorous dance form performed at Hindu shrines, often depicting the story of Veerabhadra, an avatar of Shiva.

Jangam Tradition

A part of the larger Veerashaiva-Lingayat tradition, emphasizing the worship of Lord Shiva and the importance of the Lingam.
